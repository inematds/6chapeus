What it is When to use Flow Rules Synthesis
skill · fs-six-hats

Six Hats + Anti-Anchor

De Bono's method, but with two layers that make it operable by Claude: mandatory bias rupture before thinking and strict isolation between phases. One hat at a time, no cross-contamination.

01

The idea in one sentence

Instead of mixing facts, risks, emotions, and alternatives in the same head, the skill forces you to wear a single hat at a time. Each phase is monolithic. Blue opens and closes. In between, one single mode of thinking.

The result is not a decorative "pros and cons" list. It's an actionable recommendation with a Plan B, review metrics, and declared gaps.

02

When to trigger and when not to

Use for

  • Hard or poorly reversible decisions
  • Team conflicts or tangled tensions
  • Validating a technical or commercial proposal
  • A project pre-mortem
  • Breaking out of a thinking block

Don't use for

  • Simple factual questions
  • Pure execution (code, email, copy)
  • Trivial and reversible decisions
  • When you want a quick opinion without process
  • If the problem fits in 3 lines
03

The full flow, phase by phase

Eight phases in order. Phase 0 is mandatory even when no anchor is detected. Blue appears twice: to open and to close with synthesis.

0

Phase 0 · Anti-Anchor

Before the hats. Pure reformulation of the problem, identification of the invisible assumption, steel-man of the opposite, and a quick pre-mortem. Breaks both your initial bias and mine.

mandatory
🎩

Blue Hat · Opening

Defines the exact reformulated question, the known constraints, the success criteria, and the chosen hat order. If context is missing, stops and asks.

4-8 lines
⚪

White Hat · Facts

Data only. Separated into verified facts, believed-but-unverified facts, and information gaps. Nothing invented. If critical data is missing, it's declared.

zero opinion
☀️

Yellow Hat · Benefits

Between 3 and 7 benefits, each with its own logical backing. Forbidden to hedge with "although" or include warnings. That belongs to Black.

with a "why"
⚫

Black Hat · Risks

Negative logic, not pessimism. Between 3 and 7 concrete risks with probability, impact, and reasoning. Be severe. This is the most valuable hat and the one most diluted by complacency.

no softening
🌱

Green Hat · Creative alternatives

At least 5 executively distinct alternatives, using at least 3 divergent frameworks and one radical provocation. No judging here: that comes later.

5+ / 3 frameworks
❤️

Red Hat · Intuition

Emotional reactions without justification. Short, direct, visceral sentences. If "because" appears in a sentence, the sentence is invalid. Max 8 sentences.

no "because"
🎩

Blue Hat · Synthesis

Convergence with a decision matrix. One actionable recommendation, a Plan B with activation condition, review metrics at 1-3 months, and the gaps that remain open.

one option
04

The rules that don't move

01 · ISOLATION

One hat at a time. While you're wearing one, the others don't exist. If content from another shows up, discard it or save it for its phase.

02 · OPENING AND CLOSING

Blue always opens and closes. No session starts without an opening Blue. No session closes without a synthesis.

03 · CLEAN RED

No justification in Red. If "because" appears, the sentence is invalid and gets rewritten.

04 · VERIFIABLE BLACK

Black is negative logic, not pessimism. Only real risks, not "I don't like it". That's Red.

05 · YELLOW WITH LOGIC

Every benefit needs its own logical defense. Enthusiasm without an argument doesn't count.

06 · DIVERGENT GREEN

Green uses divergence frameworks. At least three distinct ones and at least one radical provocation.

07 · DON'T MIX

Don't switch hats mid-phase. If the phase runs short, close it and move on to the next.

08 · DECLARED EMPTINESS

If there's no material for a phase, declare it. "I find no solid risks" is valuable information. Don't fill with fake content.

05

What the final synthesis delivers

  1. Map of the terrain. 3-5 lines summarizing what was seen after the six hats.
  2. Detected tensions. Where Black and Yellow collide, where Red doesn't fit the logic.
  3. Decision matrix. 3-5 problem-specific criteria, with the Green alternatives scored.
  4. Actionable recommendation. One prioritized option with concrete steps. One, not three.
  5. Plan B. Second option with an explicit activation condition.
  6. Review metrics. Concrete evidence that would make you revisit the choice in 1-3 months.
  7. Pending gaps. White Hat information still missing and that should be obtained before executing.

How to invoke: just ask for "six hats", "seis chapeus", "seis chapéus", "multi-perspective analysis", "help me think", or "break the anchor". Also triggers on a "should I do X?" when emotional, technical, and strategic tensions are mixed together.
